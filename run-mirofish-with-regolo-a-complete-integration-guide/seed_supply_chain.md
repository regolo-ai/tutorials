# Supply Chain Crisis: Semiconductor Disruption Simulation

**Market Research Report — Taiwan Strait Shipping Disruption (Q1 2026)**

## Executive Summary

A 60-day shipping blockade in the Taiwan Strait has halted the export of approximately **60% of the world's advanced semiconductor output**. TSMC, ASE Group, and MediaTek fulfillment hubs at Kaohsiung and Keelung ports are confirmed inaccessible. Spot-market lead times for 3nm–7nm logic chips have spiked from an average of 12 weeks to **52+ weeks**. NAND flash and DRAM spot prices have increased 34% in the first 14 days.

## Affected Parties

**Upstream (manufacturing):**  TSMC (>50% global foundry revenue), UMC, GlobalFoundries Taiwan operations, ASE packaging.

**Midstream (distribution):** Spot brokers in Singapore, South Korea, and the Netherlands have activated force-majeure clauses. Air-freight rates for wafer-level packages are up 8x baseline.

**Downstream (OEMs/brands):** European consumer electronics, automotive Tier-1 suppliers (Bosch, Continental, ZF), payment terminal vendors (Ingenico, Verifone, Atos), and industrial automation firms (Siemens, ABB, Schneider Electric) have all issued earnings guidance warnings.

## Geopolitical Context

The blockade follows a series of escalating military exercises. Three diplomatic channels are active: US–China bilateral track, EU trade mission to Beijing, and ASEAN-mediated framework. Analysts assign a **35% probability** to a resolution within 60 days and a **55% probability** to an 18-month sustained disruption.

## Strategic Questions for European Industry

1. Can European manufacturers absorb the shock using existing buffer inventory?
2. Which nearshoring alternatives (Intel Fab 34 in Ireland, Infineon Dresden, STMicro Crolles) can realistically absorb demand in 12–18 months?
3. What financial hedges and insurance instruments are viable in this environment?
4. How should firms communicate with investors and customers about delivery timelines?

## Data Points

- **Intel Fab 34 (Leixlip, Ireland):** Capacity: ~20K wafer starts/month at 7nm EUV. Backlog: 18 months.
- **Infineon Dresden expansion:** Ramping 300mm power semiconductors. Online Q3 2026. Not suitable for advanced logic.
- **EU Chips Act commitments:** €43B pledged; manufacturing targets 2030. No near-term production uplift.
- **Japan alternative:** Rapidus fab (2nm target, 2027). Too early to qualify.
- **TSMC Arizona Fab 21:** Phase 1 online, 4nm, ~8K wafer starts/month. Heavily oversubscribed by Apple and Nvidia.

## Scenario Modelling

**Base case (55% weight):** 18-month disruption. ~40% of EU electronics SMEs face insolvency or forced consolidation. Automotive sector experiences worst chip shortage since 2021 but with longer tail. Winners: firms with direct TSMC capacity reservations and strong balance sheets.

**Bull case (35% weight):** 60-day resolution via US diplomatic pressure. Permanent demand shift toward non-Taiwan sources accelerates. Long-term structural change but immediate crisis averted.

**Bear case (10% weight):** Escalation to active conflict. Full Taiwan manufacturing offline for 24+ months. No current global alternative exists. Catastrophic for European tech sector.
